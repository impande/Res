ATS Resume Builder — FAQs

What is ATS and why does it matter?

ATS stands for Applicant Tracking System — software used by over 97% of Fortune 500 companies and most modern employers to automatically scan, filter, and rank resumes before a human ever reads them. If your resume uses complex formatting, tables, graphics, or lacks the right keywords, ATS can reject it outright even if you are perfectly qualified. An ATS-optimised resume ensures your application reaches a real recruiter.

How do I make my resume ATS-friendly?

Use a clean, single-column layout with no tables, text boxes, or images. Use standard section headings like Work Experience, Education, and Skills. Include relevant keywords from the job description. Avoid placing critical information in headers and footers. Our ATS resume builder handles all of this automatically — every template is ATS-tested and every section uses standard formatting that parsing engines can read perfectly.

Do all templates on Resume For You pass ATS?

Yes. Every template available on Resume For You has been designed with ATS compatibility as the first priority. We avoid tables, multi-column layouts that confuse parsers, images, and decorative elements that break ATS reading. You can choose any template confident that it will be parsed correctly by all major ATS platforms.

What is an ATS score?

An ATS score is a relevancy rating that an Applicant Tracking System assigns to your resume based on how well it matches the job description — typically measured by keyword overlap, formatting readability, and completeness of standard sections. Resume For You includes a built-in ATS score checker that analyses your resume against your target job title so you can optimise before applying.
